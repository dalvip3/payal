-Yesterday's Perfume The word perfume comes from the Latin phrase, “per” meaning “thorough” and “fumus” meaning “smoke”. The French later gave the name “parfum” to the smells produced by burning incense. Indeed, the first form of perfume was incense, first made by the Mesopotamians about 4000 years ago.

-It is the ratio of alcohol to scent that determines the perfume's strength and worth. The more essential oil, the stronger (and more expensive) the perfume is. Eau de parfum will have a lower concentration of alcohol while body mists will have a much higher concentration of alcohol.

-Many perfumes are made by extracting scented oil from natural ingredients. These ingredients can include various plants, fruits, woods and even animal secretions. Other resources like alcohol, coal, tars and petrochemicals can be used during the manufacturing process.

1. Guess Brand Perfume

-Guess perfume is a women's perfume that includes a distinctly feminine take on classic scents. In the Guess scent, one finds layers of subtle undertones from the fruity scents of tangerine and apple to the floral scents of freesia, peony and muguet.

-Founded in 1981 by the Marciano brothers, Guess has produced an impressive lineup of fragrances, offering something for every occasion.

-GUESS is one of the best perfume brands that is known for exhaling seductive, floral and fruity fragrance.

2. Hermes Brand Perfume

-The first Hermès perfume was made in 1951 and is named Eau d'Hermès. It is still one of the top fragrances in French and world perfumery. It is designed for men and women. This perfume is inspired by the subtle smell of leather, delicate flowers, fresh citrus fruits, and spices.

-Hermès International S.A., or simply Hermès (/ɛərˈmɛz/ ( listen) air-MEZ, French: [ɛʁmɛs] ( listen)), is a French luxury design house established in 1837. It specializes in leather goods, lifestyle accessories, home furnishings, perfumery, jewelry, watches and ready-to-wear.

-The decision to choose and produce a perfume at Hermès is made by three people: Agnès de Villers (the General Director of Hermès Parfums), Pierre-Alexis Dumas and the perfumer, me. It's fantastic, but it's also a big responsibility.

3. Versace Brand Perfume

-Inviting and airy, the fragrance is open, inclusive and designed with the Versace woman in mind. Notes are natural and fresh, with pops of pomegranate, shining yuzu and a floral finish.

-The Versace logo is the head of Medusa, a Greek mythological figure. The logo came from the floor of ruins in the area of Reggio Calabria that the Versace siblings played in as children. Gianni Versace chose Medusa as the logo because she made people fall in love with her and they had no way back.

-Versace is indeed a luxury brand, founded in the late 1970s by Gianni Versace, and developed with the future help of Donatella Versace. The revolutionary designs were something different from what the fashion lovers have seen before.

4. Creed Brand Perfume

-Creed is currently the only major perfume house that makes use of traditional methods, mostly natural ingredients, and only very high-quality synthetics to create and craft their perfumes. Exceptional quality is one reason the House of Creed charge a premium for their perfume collection.

-Founded in London in 1760, Creed is a fragrance brand that has served royal houses and the discerning public for over 250 years. Led by sixth-generation master perfumer Olivier Creed and his son, Erwin, Paris-based Creed crafts timeless perfumes using the finest natural essences from around the world.
